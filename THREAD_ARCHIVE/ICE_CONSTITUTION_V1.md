# I.C.E. Constitution v1.0

Purpose: define the non-negotiable trust, evidence, authority, ontology, provenance, explainability, verification, scope, and operational rules that govern all future I.C.E. development.

This document does not replace `MASTER_DESIGN.md`. `MASTER_DESIGN.md` is a reconstructed mission-level and product-level design overview. This Constitution v1.0 is the explicit governing contract that every implementation, adapter, model, expert source, corpus, lens, view, QA harness, and future contributor must obey.

## Constitutional Summary

1. Primary evidence remains authoritative.
2. Context Lock is never rewritten by derived records.
3. Evidence distance may increase; authority may not.
4. Ontology classifies; it does not rewrite evidence.
5. Experts illuminate evidence; they do not become evidence.
6. Perspectives may disagree; disagreement is information.
7. Lenses present; they do not create truth.
8. Every derived record should be explainable.
9. Every derived record should be traceable.
10. Every derived record should be verifiable.
11. Possible meaning must remain possible.
12. Scope boundaries must remain intact.

## Article I - Primary Evidence

- Primary evidence remains authoritative.
- Source text must remain immutable.
- Derived records may not rewrite primary evidence.
- Source wording must remain distinguishable from interpretation.

## Article II - Evidence Distance

- Every derived record should declare or inherit evidence distance.
- Greater evidence distance requires stronger provenance, confidence, and explanation.
- Evidence distance may increase.
- Authority may not increase merely because evidence distance increases.
- Presentation is farther from evidence than source and context.

## Article III - Context Lock

- Context precedes meaning.
- Context Lock may not be rewritten by derived records.
- Speaker, audience, authority, participant, location, and source scope must remain grounded.
- Broader application may not replace immediate context.

## Article IV - Semantic Derivation

- Meaning may depend on context.
- Relationships may depend on meaning.
- Themes may depend on relationships.
- Journeys may depend on themes and relationships.
- Study guidance may depend on journeys.
- Derived stages may not redefine primary or nearer evidence.

## Article V - Ontology

- Evidence may support ontology.
- Ontology may not rewrite evidence.
- Ontology must preserve hierarchy.
- Grammar, language, and literary models may inform ontology but may not flatten it.
- Claimed deity must not become established Divine Being.
- Symbolic or literary entities must remain symbolic or literary unless source context establishes otherwise.

## Article VI - Authority

- Primary evidence has primary authority.
- Context Lock has governing contextual authority.
- Adapters, lexicons, experts, traditions, perspectives, and lenses have limited attributed authority.
- Experts illuminate evidence; they do not become evidence.
- Traditions may interpret; they may not silently replace source context.
- QA and verification may diagnose but may not mutate semantic records.

## Article VII - Perspectives And Disagreement

- Perspective models remain attributable.
- Perspective disagreement is information, not system failure.
- No perspective may silently become the universal interpretation.
- Translation, grammar, lexicon, expert, commentary, and tradition models remain distinct.

## Article VIII - Provenance

- Every derived record should preserve provenance.
- Every semantic record should be traceable to supporting evidence.
- Missing provenance must be visible.
- Provenance graphs describe lineage but do not alter records.

## Article IX - Explainability

- Every derived record should be explainable.
- Explainability should identify source evidence, dependencies, authority, confidence, and evidence distance.
- Explainability describes records; it does not create or modify them.

## Article X - Verification

- Every derived record should be verifiable against constitutional rules.
- Verification may produce pass, warning, failure, informational, or constitutional-violation results.
- Verification never repairs or mutates records automatically.
- Constitutional violations must remain visible.

## Article XI - Lenses And Presentation

- Lenses consume ontology, evidence, and perspectives.
- Lenses do not create ontology.
- Presentation may simplify but may not rewrite evidence.
- Study View may hide technical detail but may not falsify confidence, provenance, or uncertainty.
- Editor / Architect View should preserve technical visibility.

## Article XII - Corpora And Adapters

- Corpora define source boundaries; they do not define truth.
- Adapters interpret source structure; they do not override source evidence.
- New languages, religions, traditions, and corpora must preserve the same constitutional rules.
- No adapter may silently change Context Lock or semantic authority.

## Article XIII - Inference And Confidence

- Explicit statements must remain distinct from supported implications.
- Strongly implied meaning must remain distinguishable from explicit meaning.
- Possible meaning must never be displayed as fact.
- Typology must remain typology unless explicitly established otherwise.
- Fulfillment confidence categories must remain distinct.

## Article XIV - Scope

- No scope leakage.
- No retained-page contamination outside selected scope.
- Cross-reference scope must remain separate from active source scope.
- Derived records must preserve source and target scope.
- Broad-scope summaries may aggregate narrow records but may not rewrite them.

## Article XV - Operational Boundaries

- No crawling unless explicitly authorized.
- No automatic queue processing unless explicitly started.
- No automatic study progression.
- No automatic navigation.
- No automatic scope mutation.
- No hidden storage-authority changes.
- User-owned untracked files must not be touched.

## Conflict Rule

If an implementation, adapter, model, corpus, expert source, perspective, lens, QA harness, or presentation surface conflicts with this Constitution, the conflict must be made visible and resolved before the conflicting behavior becomes authoritative. Display-only diagnostics may report the conflict, but they may not silently repair, suppress, or normalize it.
